About Diabetic Retinopathy

Diabetic retinopathy is a complication of diabetes that affects the eyes, caused by damage to the blood vessels of the retina, the light-sensitive tissue at the back of the eye.

Causes

  • Chronically high blood sugar levels
  • High blood pressure (hypertension)
  • High cholesterol (hyperlipidemia)
  • Duration of diabetes

Symptoms

  • Blurred vision
  • Floaters (spots or dark strings in vision)
  • Fluctuating vision
  • Vision loss

Treatment Options

  • Laser therapy (photocoagulation)
  • Anti-VEGF injections
  • Vitrectomy surgery in advanced cases
